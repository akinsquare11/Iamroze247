A NEW

Over time things change,
Things change as time passes,
Tick Tick Tick says the clock,
As so time passes as flows,
It flows as the stream of time,
It flows never ending,
And never to return to its origin.

At the point of start it denies restart,
Once an action is set: it is set,
And it stays set,
It stays set that way forever,
At least forever.

Once in that stream I was born,
Once in that stream you were born,
Once in that stream our parts were made,
Our parts were made to cross,
To cross and determine destiny,
A destiny to tag life,
Either as the error of time,
Or rather the invention of time,
The history of tomorrow,
And once in that stream we shall all die.

Once in that stream I was born,
Once an innocent lad an infant,
I crawled and sat,
I stood and fell,
I stood and began to walk,
I walked and began to run,
I ran and began to swim,
I began to swim in the stream of life.

I watched helplessly
As seconds passed and became minutes,
Minutes passed and became hours,
Hours passed and became days,
Days passed and became weeks,
Weeks passed and became months,
Months passed and became years,
Each time it revolves I revolve with it,
And celebrate it as a NEW YEAR.

Seasons passed,
After summer came winter,
And each time it presented a gift,
It presented a new year,
But what does this mean?

Each time I heard 
A new year a new life I marvelled,
Is it a continuous process? 
Or it is a process of reborn?
Is it what I say it is?
Or how I move with it?
Is it good or evil?
Is it change?
Is change a bad or a good thing?
None scarves me as the thought
“the older I get the closer I am to my death”

A day has gone passed,
We are all started a new,
We rejoiced and drank wine,
I went home I slept I woke,
It was another day,
How funny time moves,
As it moves you loose track of it.

Each time we get thought time is of the essence,
A second might be small but it makes a minute,
A minute might be small but it makes an hour,
Hours make days,
And there are 365 days in a year.

I reflect on the stream of time,
There are two things to it,
It is either your memories are washed away in time,
Or it stays as the current that drives the stream.

Do you want to get washed away in time?
Or stand as the current that drives it?
Do you want to tell a dead story?
Or have someone tell a great tale of you?
A great man dies one day,
So also a foolish one,
The foolish memories dies with the fool,
But that of the wise lives on as his legacy.
ABANOUB S.
