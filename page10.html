SO LONG A CALL

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                The memories burn fresh,
It hurts deep in my flesh,
For a moment sad,
A moment I wished I had,
To hold and to cherish,
The moment stress
Of the love we had,
Once more to behold.

So long a call,
So long ago,
When it was just us,
A perfect pair admired by all,
So I was told,
Just before we crashed.

We stood still on seas like cargoes,
Still just as much as they loved our tango,
I was your king and you my queen,
You were my sun and I your rainbow.

The memories burn fresh,
For sometime it stretched,
The first time we met
Like the last cream we ate,
The sun had her eyes high,
And the trees grooving,
Like the birds on fresh feeds,
We were set on fresh fields,
I was that frightened hero,
Acting like a brave coward,
Forward and forward I went,
I stopped in front of a hill,
The most beautiful hill
I’ve seen so long ago.

They called me stupid,
Though it was all cupid,
Though it seems so today,
But I am glad it did.

Stirring in the face of time,
I beseech you,
Oh gracious fellow,
She that stands as the definition of beauty,
Though not faultless but flawless.

I see red all over,
Like roses it rovers,
It’s the season for cupid,
But also the season for a nice lad,
A sacrificial lamb,
One that acts as lovers lamp,
With red flames it burns never over,
I beseech thee,
Oh gracious fellow,
With all the gladness and love,
I wish you a happy valentine. 
ABANOUB S.
